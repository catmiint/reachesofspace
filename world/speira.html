The Speira System

The Speira system is a notably interesting locale, spiralling through space in orbit around the remains of a dead star formerly referred to as Sahar. The orbit the whole system takes is growing looser as external gravities slowly pull it off the course it remains on, but there’s somehow some remainder of force from Sahar to keep it going in orbit - at least, for now.

The heart of the Speira System is a white dwarf star known as RX-013 on Earth, but more locally known by the name Astraura. The light it sheds is minimal due to its size, causing some of the planets to develop their own light in the form of bioluminescence. It gives off just enough heat to keep the three planets orbiting it habitable, which is honestly astounding for such a weak star.

Planet Type Water-based
Habitability Habitable (only for aquatic life)
Closeness to Sun Consistantly ~0.015 AU (Usually the Closest)
Flora + Fauna Marble has mass amounts of natural flora and fauna, mostly being made up of microorganisms. Several different algae, zooplankton, and bacteria have been found flourishing in massive amounts. Other parts of the food chain involve fish species, the most notable of which being the Spangled Waterweaver, a long thin fish that propells itself through the water at extreme speeds, using it's sharp fins to impale possible meals.
Megafauna are far rarer, but do thrive in this enviroment. A species similar to Earth's Whales exists, filterfeeding via several mouth like openings covering their massive bodies. These creatures work like moving microbiomes, with plantlife forming on their sides due to their extremely slow movement and nutrient rich excrement.
Weather The weather of Marble tends to be peaceful, almost always sunny days and a permanent warm summer, usually spiking at 37 degrees celsius, on the hottest recorded days. Though, things change when a new moon is pulled into orbit. More often than not, when a new moon is pulled in, so are several meteoroids, causing meteor showers that can be seen even from the bottom of the planet's ocean. These meteors carry the stardust the planet is so rich in, and restocks the planet with it's magic. While their landing places in the ocean may be heavily disturbed at first, the nature always bounces back in a matter of weeks, thriving off of the nutrient rich space rocks.
Geography Geography is rather barren above the water, as the only apparent things up top are cities enchanted to float on top of the water, where gummisaurs and terrestrial species live most of their lives. The cities are encapsulated in a magic force field, keeping breathable air inside, as the air outside is so rich in oxygen it can cause oxygen toxicity, similar to what human divers experience when breathing too much oxygen under higher pressure levels.
Under the water is where most of the planet's life hides, on rocky stretches of underwater hydrothermal vents, to giant reefs with the towering coral fashioned into homes for aquatic Gummisaurs, if you wish to see natural wonders, you look deep deep down.
Moons In a surprising twist for a planet this large, Marble doesn't have any permanent moons! Any spaces rocks caught by it's gravity during orbit tend to go their own seperate ways after a couple of years. Because of this, many Gummisaurs station telescopes pointing towards the sky, watching the moons switch. This of course changes the tides of the planet, and most floating cities are equipped to be tossed around when the tides are changed drasitically. During these moons switches, or time without a moon, celebrations and events are prevalent, most commonly being mourning of the last moon, and the welcoming of the latest.
Major Imports/Exports A major Export form AB-56239 is Stardust! It's found in high concentrations inside the planet's luminescent water! Originally these effects were presumed to be bioluminescent algae blooms, but further study shows that it's stardust from several nearby stars imploding. The stardust has to be strained from the water before being bottled for average consumption, but Gummisaurs who live under the water have been shown get hungry less often, attributed to the stardust being absorbed in small amounts via their gummi while swimming!
Planetary Mythos The most major mythos of the planet is what gives it it's nickname, Aquarius' Marble. It's said that ages ago, the Goddess Aquarius was born in the deepest depths of Marble, laid in a bed of soft seaweed. Her birth shook the entire planet, magic bursting at it's seems, and throwing the planet's moon away. Distressed the ocean creatures begged Aquarius to return it. The sky was dark and the waters still entirely, the planet sick without it's beloved moon. To remedy this, Aquarius reached from the waters, and pulled another space rock into place, the planet returning to normalcy. Now, every few years, it's said that Aquarius's power pushes the new moon away, and she instead carries in a new moon for the planet she was born from.
Aquarius' form is entirely unknown, other than being described as feminine, and giant, with enough magical strength to change celestial objects in the sky. She is now said to reside in the deepest crevice of the ocean, only awakening from her slumber to return a moon to it's place.
Magic Capacity/Phenomena Magic is abundant on Marble due to it's high stardust count, most notably luminescent magic. While several animals on the planet show natural bioluminescence, the vast majority will produce their own light via stardust consumption. As the animals swim, their gills take in the stardust in the water, resulting in super speed, glowing, and other vibrant defense mechanisms. This also means the plant life grows swiftly, giant algae blooms lighting up the entire planet from space, thriving due to the oxygen rich air and magic rich water.

Planet Name Meaning together in Aerish, implying the strong integrity of the planet itself
Planet Type Terrestrial
Habitability Habitable, with a similar atmosphere to Earth, albeit with a slightly lower gravity level.
Closeness to Sun Orbit is lopsided and thus shuffles between being 0.01 AU (the closest) and 0.017 AU (second closest)
Flora/Fauna Lots of colorful and unusually shaped plants dot across the grasslands and jungle terrain of the planet; and almost all flora grow to be extremely large due to the high oxygen levels present in the air. Fauna consist of brightly colored and equally as large creatures mimicking those seen on Earth with similar gilled attachments to the Aeris.
Sentient Life Alien-like humans, called Aeris - they appear to closely resemble what humans "act" like, though their ears and noses are replaced with gills for filtering air and they may have unnatural hair and eye colors. As a whole, they are rather friendly and live in a mutually beneficial agreement across the Aera land.
Weather The weather is rather windy and sometimes very humid as the planet orbits a nearby sun, but all life on AERA have grown accustomed to the normal temperatures present.
Geography Natural forests, jungles, as well as pure-white deserts are a common sight in AERA. There are a few sunken isles (present by the darker toned blues), the most notable being the largest piece of land lost to the depths but still very much alive by the vegetation and marine life.
Moons s shown in the image, AERA has two moons that cycle both night and day: the smaller one is named NEERA, and the larger, MEGAS. They were pulled by the strong asteroid belt that circles the planet and has since created a natural orbit for both of the moons.
Planetary Mythos There is a belief that spirits watch over the planet and were the cause of the sunken isles where they are believed to reside. It is something that many Aeris trust in wholeheartedly and actively seek to stay in the good favors of the spirits above. Some claim they reeled in and captured the moons to cause the water to sink those isles.
Magic Capacity/Phenomena The unique asteroid belt surrounding AERA is capable of creating bright and sparkling meteor showers as every month is passed. It appears like fireworks and shimmers of light bursting across the planet's deep sky. And although this magical occurence happens normally for the Aeris on the planet, they have little magic capacity with no special or significant powers. The fauna and flora, however, seem to emit similar magical capabilities as the mysterious asteroids that encompass AERA.

AERA is the planet that the gummisaur Kawa-Taro resides in, the planet he considers his home. Soon after being born, Kawa-Taro came across the planet AERA and made himself comfortable with the extremely kind Aeris there. He found his love for cucumbers and kabocha on AERA, and although he visits nearby planets in the galaxy it belongs, Kawa-Taro loves hoarding his favorite things on this planet especially as its shelf life is considerably longer than on other lands. Kawa-Taro is indifferent to the Aeris but will not hesitate to help if any come his way, word gets around when an odd creature can communicate and offer guidance and stories of the vast spaces beyond AERA. His experience on AERA is a familial and protective one, as Kawa-Taro holds this planet near and dear.
